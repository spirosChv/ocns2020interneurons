Alexandra Tzilivaki (Primary Organizer)

Follow @TwitterDev

Einstein Center for Neurosciences (ECN), Charite Medical School, Berlin, Germany.

Short bio

Alexandra Tzilivaki, received her BSc and MSc in Biology, with honors, from the University of Crete in Greece. She is currently a PhD candidate researcher in the laboratory of Prof. Dr. Dietmar Schmitz in Charite Medical School Berlin in Germany. She is also a member of Panayiota Poirazi Laboratory in IMBB FORTH in Crete. Alexandra is especially interested in understanding the contribution of interneurons in learning and memory processes with an emphasis to dendritic functions and hippocampal oscillations, via the utilization of modelling and experimental approaches. Her work has been supported among others by the Einstein Foundation, by the Onassis Foundation and by Google.

Dr. Spiros Chavlis (co-organizer)

Follow @TwitterDev

Institute of Molecular Biology and Biotechnology, Foundation for Research and Technology Hellas, Heraklion, Greece

Short bio

Spiros Chavlis, received his MEng from the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA) and his MSc from Imperial College London. He has received his PhD from University of Crete, specialized in Computationa Neuroscience. He is currently a postdoctoral researcher in Panayiota's Poirazi Laboratory in IMBB, FORTH in Crete, Greece. Spiros is interested in understanding the role of large scale network dynamics in spatial memory encoding and in improving the current state-o-the-art Deep Learning architectures incorporating rules from the mammalian brain. Her work has been supported among others by the Stavros Niarchos Foundation.
